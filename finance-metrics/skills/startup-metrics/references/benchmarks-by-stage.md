# Startup Benchmarks by Stage

Detailed benchmarks and target metrics by funding stage (Pre-seed, Seed, Series A, Series B). Includes variations by business model (SaaS, Marketplace, Consumer, B2B) and geography (US, Europe, Korea).

## Overview

### Stage-Specific Focus

| Stage | Primary Goal | Core Question | Investor Focus |
|-------|-------------|----------------|-----------------|
| **Pre-seed** | Problem-solution fit | "Are you solving a real problem?" | Team, market size, early traction |
| **Seed** | Product-Market Fit | "Do people want this?" | Retention, unit economics, growth rate |
| **Series A** | Scalable Growth Engine | "Is it repeatable?" | Channel efficiency, CAC Payback, MRR growth |
| **Series B** | Efficient Growth | "Is there a path to profitability?" | Rule of 40, Burn Multiple, market share |

- **Post-money Valuation**: $5-15M (US), ₩50-150B (Korea)

## Geographic Differences (Korea vs US)

### Korea Market Characteristics

| Dimension | Korea | US | Rationale |
|-----------|-------|----|-----------| 
| **Valuation** | 20-30% lower | Baseline | Market size, exit possibilities |
| **Funding Size** | 30-50% smaller | Baseline | LP pool size, fund size |
| **CAC** | 50-70% lower | Baseline | Channel costs, wage levels |
| **ACV** | 40-60% lower | Baseline | Enterprise IT budgets, willingness to pay |
| **Sales Cycle** | 1.5-2x longer | Baseline | Decision process, risk aversion |
| **Churn Rate** | 1.5-2x higher | Baseline | Startup failure rate, economic sensitivity |

### Korea Benchmark Adjustment Guide

**SaaS (Series A example)**:
- ARR: $1.5M → ₩15-20B (FX + 30% discount)
- ARPA: $500 → ₩500K-700K/mo
- CAC Payback: <18mo → <24mo acceptable
- NRR: >100% → >95% acceptable (high churn expected)

**B2B**:
- ACV: $50K → ₩50-70M (FX + 20% discount)
- Sales Cycle: 90d → 120-150d
- Win Rate: 25% → 20% (longer sales cycle)

**Marketplace**:
- Take Rate: 20% → 15-18% (competition, payment culture)
- GMV Growth: 3x → 2.5x (market size)

**Consumer**:
- ARPU: $1 → ₩800-1,000/mo (low willingness to pay)
- Monetization %: 5% → 3-4% (premium resistance)

## Benchmark Usage Cautions

### 1. Context Matters
- **Industry differences**: FinTech vs EdTech vs HealthTech all vary
- **Business model**: PLG vs sales-led, self-serve vs white-glove
- **Target segment**: SMB vs mid-market vs enterprise

### 2. Trends Matter More Than Absolutes
- **Improvement trajectory**: MoM/QoQ improvement > absolute numbers
- **Cohort analysis**: Recent cohorts outperforming prior ones
- **Leading indicators**: Track leading over lagging indicators

### 3. Benchmarks Aren't Goals
- **Own data first**: Improvement vs past performance is primary goal
- **Achievability**: Unattainable benchmarks demoralize teams
- **Investor communication**: You're OK if you can explain why you differ

### 4. Definition Consistency
- **Standardize calculation**: NRR, churn definitions vary by company
- **Align timing**: MRR month-end? Average? Define clearly
- **Exclusion rules**: Test customers, employee discounts, etc.

### 5. Composite Metrics
- **Avoid single-metric traps**: MRR alone misses churn
- **Rule of 40**: Balance growth and profitability
- **Burn Multiple**: Comprehensive efficiency indicator
- **Unit Economics**: CAC, LTV, payback triangle validation
